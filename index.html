    773 Million people around the world are illiterate.

Currently, 773 million people around the world are illiterate who cannot read and write (UNESCO, 2021). These people are at a significant disadvantage to get jobs and make a living to meet basic needs. According to Statista (2020), many places around the world (e.g. Sub-Saharan African 34.7%, South Asia 27.1%, and the Arab States 20.6%) have a very high percentage of illiteracy rate. For example, 81% of adults in Niger and 68% of adults in South Sudan cannot read and write (Our World in Data, 2018). Sadly, illiteracy is a problem in many countries.

Illiterate adults are more likely to be in poverty

Illiterate adults are more likely to be unemployed
