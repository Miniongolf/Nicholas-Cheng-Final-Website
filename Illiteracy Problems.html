Currently, 773 million people around the world are illiterate (UNESCO, 2021).

Illiterate adults are more likely to:

  • Be unemployed
  • Have a low income
  • Live in poverty
  • Have low self-esteem
  • Become teenage parents
  • Miss out on opportunities to participate fully in society and the workforce
  • Not understand essential information in their daily lives
  • Have workplace accidents
  • Take longer to recover from injury/disease
  • Misuse medication
  • Lack basic needs (e.g. adequate shelter and/or nutrition)

Illiteracy costs the global economy $1.5 trillion annually because literacy is the foundation of community and economic development. Studies show that 85% of all teenagers involved in criminal activities are functionally illiterate, and high crime rates have a direct relation to low literacy skills (World Literacy Foundation, 2021). Finding a solution to help with the global illiteracy problem is an urgent task so that issues such as unemployment due to lack of education, poverty issues, lack of nutrition, high death rate, high crime rate, and high workplace accidents can be improved. Helping more people around the world to be literate allows those individuals to have the opportunities to participate fully in society and the workforce. Becoming literate significantly improves a person's economic opportunities, increase self-esteem, provides benefits of health and safety, and strengthens one's relationships and civic engagement (HuffPost, 2017). The society as a whole benefits from a more literate populations as more money goes into the economy, while criminal justice and education system costs decrease.
